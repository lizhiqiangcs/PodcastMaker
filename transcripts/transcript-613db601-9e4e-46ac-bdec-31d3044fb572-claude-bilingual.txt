Speaker 2-3 [倾斜 vs 扭角]:
EN: **Inclination:** Simpler concept. Moving entire center of mass to inside of turn to balance against lateral forces. Similar to leaning motorcycle/bicycle into turn - whole body leans as one unit. Feet tip, knees tip, hips tip, shoulders follow. Entire body leaning into hill. Degree of inclination directly proportional to speed and turn radius. Faster speed and tighter arcs require more lean. Critical error: using only upper body inclination (folding over at waist without enough tipping from feet/legs). Classic "A-frame stance." Only leaning torso might feel balanced momentarily, but feet aren't tipping skis enough to generate required edge angle - results in minimal edge engagement, requiring skidding to complete turn.

**Angulation:** More complex, higher performance. Creating angles between body parts. Subtle separation necessary to achieve higher edge angle while maintaining center of mass stability. Two key types:
- **Knee Angulation:** Lower leg tipped more aggressively than upper leg, creating angle at knee joint. Allows quick fine adjustments of edge angle. Crucial in short turns where no time for massive inclination. Localized tipping movement initiated primarily by foot and ankle inside boot.
- **Hip Angulation:** Legs tipped significantly more than upper body, creating necessary angle at hip socket. Vital for high-performance turns needing aggressive edge angle and high inclination while maintaining upper body centered over base of support for balance/stability. Fighter jet pilot resistance - allows skier to counteract lateral force without collapsing inward.

Controlling edges isn't one single movement - complex interwoven dynamic of three fundamental skills working at once. Tipping of legs never isolated - it's combination of: 1) Tipping feet and lower legs (angulation), 2) Flexing knees and hips (pressure magnitude control), 3) Rotational input of steering legs. Critical for two reasons: 1) Extremely precise fine-tuned control over edge angle throughout entire arc, 2) By distributing turning force across multiple flexed and rotated joints, helps skier avoid putting too much direct shearing lateral stress on knee joint (major consideration at high speeds).
CN: **倾斜：**更简单的概念。将整个质心移到转弯内侧以平衡横向力。类似于将摩托车/自行车倾斜进入转弯——整个身体作为一个单元倾斜。脚倾斜，膝盖倾斜，髋部倾斜，肩膀跟随。整个身体向山坡倾斜。倾斜度与速度和转弯半径成正比。更快的速度和更紧的弧线需要更多的倾斜。关键错误：仅使用上身倾斜（在腰部折叠而没有从脚/腿足够的倾斜）。经典的"A形站姿"。只倾斜躯干可能暂时感觉平衡，但脚没有足够倾斜滑雪板以产生所需的刃边角度——导致最小的刃边接合，需要打滑才能完成转弯。

**扭角：**更复杂、更高性能。在身体部位之间创建角度。在保持质心稳定的同时实现更高刃边角度所需的微妙分离。两种关键类型：

- **髋扭角：**腿比上身显著倾斜更多，在髋关节窝处创建必要的角度。对于需要激进刃边角度和高倾斜度的高性能转弯至关重要，同时保持上身在支撑底面中心以保持平衡/稳定性。战斗机飞行员阻力——允许滑雪者抵消横向力而不向内塌陷。

控制刃边不是单一动作——三个基本技能同时工作的复杂交织动态。腿的倾斜从不孤立——它是以下的组合：1）倾斜脚和小腿（扭角），2）屈曲膝盖和髋部（压力大小控制），3）转向腿的旋转输入。关键的两个原因：1）在整个弧线中对刃边角度进行极其精确的微调控制，2）通过在多个屈曲和旋转的关节上分配转弯力，帮助滑雪者避免在膝关节上施加过多的直接剪切横向应力（在高速时的主要考虑因素）。
